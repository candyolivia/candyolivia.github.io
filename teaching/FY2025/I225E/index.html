Term 1-1 | I225E

Statistical Signal Processing


Course Information

Course Meeting Time

Lectures: 2 sessions / week, 1 hour 40 minutes / session

Objective

Basic understanding of random variables, probability theory, stochastic process, statistical signal processing, and model estimation theory.

Evaluation
  • Reports (20%): Four times (each 5%)
  • Examinations (80%): Two times (each 40%). Bring only your own "handwritten" notebooks (NO printed handouts, calculator, textbook, computer, or smartphone).
Staffs

Lecturers: Masashi Unoki, Candy Olivia Mawalim

Teaching assistants: Taiyang Guo, Xiajie Zhou

References
  • A. Papoulis, "Probability, random variables, and stochastic processes." (McGraw-Hill, 1991).
  • Steven M. Kay, "Fundamentals of Statistical Signal Processing." (Prentice Hall, 1993).
